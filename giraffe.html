GIRAFFE

The giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis) is the tallest terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. Known for its extremely long neck and legs, and unique coat patterns, it feeds primarily on acacia leaves high in trees. Found across African savannahs, giraffes are social and engage in "necking" battles for dominance. They are prey to lions, and calves may be targeted by other predators.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

You will find the giraffes at G15, just beyond the research centre.
