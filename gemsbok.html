Gemsbok

The gemsbok (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope with striking black-and-white facial markings and long, straight horns. It is highly adapted to survive in arid environments such as deserts and dry savannahs. Native to Southern Africa, especially Namibia, Botswana, and parts of South Africa, the gemsbok feeds on grasses, roots, and tubers, obtaining moisture from its food. It is a symbol of strength and endurance in African wildlife.

WHERE WILL YOU FIND THEM?

You will find the gemsbok in the Desert Plains area, near the arid exhibit zone.
